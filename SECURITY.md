InsureIT is a commercial vehicle insurance and claim assistance platform. It may process sensitive information such as customer details, vehicle records, policy information, claim documents, accident photographs, support tickets, staff workflows, and operational records. Protecting this information is an important responsibility for us.

If you are a security researcher, information security professional, developer, or user and you believe you have found a security vulnerability in the InsureIT website, mobile application, web portal, APIs, cloud configuration, authentication flow, storage rules, or related services, we request that you disclose it to us responsibly.

We appreciate responsible reports that help us identify and fix security issues before they can harm users, customers, partners, or the Platform.

When reporting a vulnerability, please include as much detail as possible, such as:

- affected website, app screen, API endpoint, repository path, feature, or workflow;
- vulnerability type, such as authentication bypass, access control issue, exposed data, insecure storage, file upload issue, injection, XSS, CSRF, SSRF, broken authorization, or misconfiguration;
- clear steps to reproduce the issue;
- proof of concept, screenshots, logs, or screen recording where safe to share;
- potential impact;
- whether any personal data, customer data, claim data, policy data, or documents may be affected;
- your name or organization, if you want acknowledgement;
- your preferred contact details.

Please do not include excessive personal data, customer records, policy documents, claim documents, credentials, or secrets in your report. Share only the minimum information needed for us to verify the issue.

## Our Commitment

When we receive a responsible security report, we aim to:

- acknowledge receipt within a reasonable time;
- review and validate the reported issue;
- assess severity and potential impact;
- take appropriate steps to remediate confirmed issues;
- communicate with the reporter where additional details are required;
- coordinate responsible disclosure where appropriate;
- protect users, customers, and the Platform from harm.

Response and remediation timelines may vary depending on the severity, complexity, affected systems, third-party dependencies, and operational impact.

## Scope

The following assets are generally considered in scope when owned, operated, or officially controlled by Sankalp Insurance Brokers Private Limited for the InsureIT Platform:

- InsureIT mobile application;
- InsureIT web portal;
- InsureIT website or landing pages;
- InsureIT APIs and backend services;
- authentication and role-based access flows;
- customer, vehicle, policy, claim, document, and support workflows;
- cloud storage, database security rules, and access controls;
- notification and support workflows;
- publicly accessible production or staging environments that are explicitly authorized for testing.

Do not test systems, domains, accounts, environments, or third-party services that are not owned, operated, or explicitly authorized by Sankalp Insurance Brokers Private Limited.

## Out of Scope

The following are generally out of scope unless they demonstrate a clear security impact:

- spam or social engineering reports without technical vulnerability;
- missing security headers without demonstrable impact;
- clickjacking on pages with no sensitive action;
- rate limiting issues without security impact;
- denial-of-service testing;
- physical security testing;
- social engineering of employees, customers, partners, support teams, insurers, or vendors;
- phishing attempts;
- attacks on third-party services not controlled by us;
- automated scanner output without proof of exploitability;
- self-XSS or issues requiring unrealistic user action;
- reports about outdated software without a practical exploit path;
- public information already intentionally exposed.

## Rules for Security Testing

While testing or reporting a vulnerability, you must:

- act in good faith;
- avoid harming users, customers, employees, insurers, service providers, or the Platform;
- not access, copy, modify, delete, exfiltrate, or disclose personal data, policy data, claim data, documents, photographs, payment information, or confidential business records;
- not use another person’s account without permission;
- not attempt social engineering, phishing, spam, or physical attacks;
- not perform denial-of-service, stress testing, destructive testing, or attacks that degrade service availability;
- not upload malware, ransomware, spyware, worms, or destructive payloads;
- not publicly disclose the vulnerability before we have had a reasonable opportunity to investigate and fix it;
- comply with applicable laws and regulations.

If you accidentally access sensitive data during testing, stop immediately, do not save or share the data, and report the incident to us with minimal details required for verification.

## Safe Harbor

If you make a good-faith effort to comply with this Security Policy and report a vulnerability responsibly, we will consider your report authorized for the limited purpose of vulnerability disclosure.

This does not authorize:

- access to data that does not belong to you;
- privacy violations;
- data destruction;
- service disruption;
- extortion;
- public disclosure before remediation;
- violation of applicable law;
- testing of third-party systems without authorization.

We reserve all legal rights in cases of malicious activity, fraud, extortion, abuse, unauthorized data access, or harmful conduct.

## Bug Bounty and Rewards

At present, Sankalp Insurance Brokers Private Limited has not announced a public paid bug bounty program for InsureIT unless expressly published by the Company in writing.

We may, at our sole discretion, acknowledge responsible security reports or introduce a formal bug bounty or recognition program in the future. Any reward, recognition, eligibility, scope, amount, or timing will be determined solely by the Company and will be subject to separate written rules.

Submitting a report does not automatically entitle the reporter to payment, reward, employment, contract, or public acknowledgement.

## Public Disclosure

Please do not publicly disclose, discuss, publish, post, demonstrate, or share details of a suspected vulnerability until:

- we have confirmed and remediated the issue; and
- we have provided written permission for public disclosure, where applicable.

Responsible disclosure protects customers, users, partners, and the Platform.

## Security Best Practices for Users

Users of the InsureIT Platform should:

- use strong and unique passwords;
- keep login credentials confidential;
- avoid sharing accounts;
- log out from shared devices;
- keep devices updated;
- avoid uploading unnecessary sensitive information;
- verify emails, calls, or messages claiming to be from InsureIT;
- report suspicious activity immediately;
- ensure documents uploaded to the Platform are genuine and relevant.
